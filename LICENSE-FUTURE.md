# What is FSL-1.1-MIT and how does it work?

Vepol is **source-available** software, not "open source" in the strict
[OSI](https://opensource.org/) sense. It uses the [Functional Source
License (FSL) 1.1 with MIT Future License](https://fsl.software/) — a
license developed by Sentry that combines two phases:

## Phase 1 — first 2 years (current)

While the software is fresh, FSL protects against a narrow case: someone
taking the code and offering it back as a competing managed product. Under
FSL **you can**:

- Use Vepol for any personal purpose (any device, any project)
- Use Vepol inside your company (employees, contractors)
- Modify and self-host Vepol for your own needs
- Provide professional services to clients **using** Vepol
- Fork Vepol for non-competing purposes
- Redistribute, including modified versions

Under FSL **you cannot** (without a [commercial license](COMMERCIAL.md)):

- Offer a hosted/SaaS service that competes with Vepol
- Repackage Vepol under another brand and sell it as a competing product

That's it. Everything else is permitted.

## Phase 2 — after 2 years (automatic)

**Two years after a release becomes available, that release automatically
converts to the MIT license** — the most permissive open-source license.
At that point, all restrictions disappear. The software becomes truly
open source forever.

This means:

- Vepol 0.1.0 (released April 2026) → MIT in April 2028
- Every subsequent release follows the same 2-year schedule
- The conversion is **irrevocable** and built into the license itself

## Why this license

Most commercial-friendly licenses (BUSL, Elastic v2, SSPL) have no
expiration — they stay restrictive forever. We picked FSL because:

- **Fair to users now.** Personal, commercial, and consulting use all
  free immediately. Only competing SaaS is restricted.
- **Open source eventually.** No license-rot risk. Two years out, the
  community owns the code under MIT.
- **Aligned with our funding model.** We monetize through GitHub
  Sponsors, optional Pro modules, and rare commercial licenses for
  enterprises that need pre-conversion freedom — not by holding the
  code hostage.
