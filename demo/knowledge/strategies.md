populating it. Q2 is about running it — using the brief, retro, and
backlog patterns reliably enough that they become invisible.

## Active hypotheses about Vepol

These are the demo "user's" working theories about how Vepol should help.
Each gets revisited in the weekly retro.

### H1 (active): Friday retro is the hub keystone

Hypothesis: the Friday retro produces more compounding value than any
other ritual because it forces structured reflection across all five
projects. Skipping it correlates with the next week feeling chaotic.

**Status:** holding for 12 weeks running. Two missed Fridays both
resulted in chaotic following weeks. Will continue.

### H2 (active): Three weekly priorities is the right number

Hypothesis: three weekly priorities is the maximum I can hold. Four
becomes an unprioritized to-do list; two leaves slack that gets eaten
by reactive work.

**Status:** holding for 16 weeks. Once tried four (week 8) — closed two,
felt like failure on the other two. Closing on three weekly priorities
~85% of weeks, which is the right tightness.

### H3 (active, fresh): Saturday-morning workouts beat Friday-afternoon

Hypothesis (logged 2026-04-17): in any week with active client
deliverables, Friday-afternoon workouts will get displaced. Saturday
morning is the right default.

**Status:** new hypothesis, two weeks of data so far. Will review at 30
days.

## Retired hypotheses

### H4 (retired 2026-04-13): "Open to all client types" is the right work strategy

Hypothesis from Q1: keep client mix open across SaaS, fintech, e-commerce
to find the highest-leverage segment.

**Status retired:** Q1 data showed enterprise SaaS clients paid 40%
better and consumed less attention. Continuing "open to all" was an
opportunity cost. Now focused on enterprise SaaS — see
projects/work/strategies.md.

### H5 (retired 2026-03-30): Daily journaling beats weekly retro

Hypothesis: writing every day produces better insights than concentrated
weekly reflection.

**Status retired:** tried daily for 6 weeks. Diminishing returns after
day 3 — entries became maintenance rather than reflection. Weekly with
session captures filling the daily slots is the right balance.

## Principles

- **No more than three weekly priorities.** Hard rule, not a guideline.
- **Friday retro is sacred.** Equal priority to client deliverables.
- **Every escalation gets a written response, even if the response is
  "decided later."** The decision IS the reply.
- **Health and family decisions get the same rigor as work decisions.** No
  category is treated as "soft."
- **Friction in a system is information, not annoyance.** When a workflow
  feels off three times, it goes into incidents.md as a pattern, not a
  one-off.
