# Pricing Creativity

By Tom Sant (note: there are several "Pricing Creativity" books with
similar titles by different authors). The one used here: positioning
and pricing for advisory and creative services.

Read 2026-03-25 to 2026-04-07. Targeted read in support of the Acme
renewal conversation.

## One-paragraph TL;DR

A practical book on pricing professional services. The core argument is
that pricing precedes value justification, not the other way around —
you anchor the price first and then build the case for it, rather than
build the case and let the prospect infer the price. Application is
specifically for advisory work, not productized SaaS.

## Three takeaways

### 1. Anchor first, justify after — LIFTED

Lifted to `~/knowledge/concepts/anchor-first-pricing.md` (will be added
to hub level on next sweep). Currently being tested as the Acme renewal
hypothesis — see [`projects/work/strategies.md`](../strategies.md) H2.

### 2. Three options always beat one recommendation

For decision-makers like Sarah (anchor-on-numbers, see
[`people/sarah-mendez.md`](../../../people/sarah-mendez.md)), present
three options with explicit tradeoffs rather than recommending one.
Rationale: the recommendation feels coercive; three options feel like
collaboration. The decider still picks the one we want, but feels
they chose it.

Applied to: Acme renewal SOW v2 will present three pricing tiers (hold,
+10%, +18%) with explicit scope changes per tier. Per
[`projects/work/decisions/0003-acme-renewal-pricing.md`](../decisions/0003-acme-renewal-pricing.md),
we've already chosen +18% internally — the SOW lets Sarah arrive there.

### 3. Free work is a pricing signal, not a kindness

Doing work outside the SOW for free reads to clients as "this work has
no value" — the opposite of building trust. The right move when scope
creep happens: confirm in writing that the new scope is an addition
billed at the standard rate, then deliver. Applied to: Beta-Corp 2026-04
scope creep antipattern (see project log entry 2026-04-04).
