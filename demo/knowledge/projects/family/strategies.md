# family — strategy

*Last reviewed 2026-04-13*

## Where we're going

A family that knows what it values, that rituals hold it together, and
where the parents have headspace because logistics have a system.

## Active hypotheses

### H1: Wednesday parent coffee is the highest-leverage school touchpoint

Hypothesis: a 30-minute weekly coffee with one teacher, alternating
weeks between Tom's and Lily's, produces better signal than monthly
parent-teacher conferences.

**Status:** 7 weeks running. Both teachers receptive. Two early
interventions (Tom's reading speed, Lily's distance vision) caught
weeks earlier than they would have been at conferences.

### H2: Three sustained routines beat ten attempted routines

Hypothesis: better to commit to 3 simple routines (Wed coffee, weekend
hike, screen-free dinners) and protect them than to attempt 10 and
sustain none.

**Status:** holding. All three at 4+ week streaks.

### H3 (fresh): Family decisions get the same decision-doc rigor as work

Hypothesis: writing down family decisions (with rationale, who proposed,
who agreed, what conditions would cause a re-evaluation) reduces
re-litigation 6 months later.

**Status:** new — 2 decision docs so far. Will review at 6 months.

## Retired hypotheses

### H4 (retired 2026-03-15): Joint family meetings every Sunday

Hypothesis: weekly all-four family meetings on Sunday evenings.

**Status retired:** kids found them tedious; meetings became performative.
Replaced with informal weekend dinner check-ins.

## Principles

- **No surprise schedule changes.** If a routine moves, both adults know
  by Wednesday morning at the latest.
- **One adult one decision.** Joint decisions are ratified, not made,
  in joint conversations. Pre-thinking happens individually.
- **Kids' time is theirs unless we're explicitly using it.** Default
  to "kids decide their afternoon" rather than over-scheduling.
