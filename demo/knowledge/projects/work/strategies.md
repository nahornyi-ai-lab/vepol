# work — strategy

*Last reviewed 2026-04-13*

## Where we're going

A focused enterprise-SaaS technical advisory practice. Three to five
active clients at any time, $400K annual revenue ceiling (deliberate —
above this, full-time hire required). One part-time hire by end of Q2
2026 to absorb admin overhead. NPS-equivalent of 9+ across clients.

## Active hypotheses

### H1 (validated 2026-04-13): Enterprise-SaaS focus pays better

Hypothesis: tightening client mix to enterprise-SaaS will produce both
higher revenue per client and less attention drain.

**Status:** validated by Q1 data. Enterprise-SaaS clients (Acme, last
year's two-client engagement) paid 40% more per hour and required ~30%
fewer non-billable touchpoints than the SMB / fintech mix from previous
quarters. Now operationalized — LinkedIn update pending.

### H2 (active): Anchor pricing before justifying

Hypothesis (after reading Tom Sant's *Pricing Creativity*): leading the
proposal with the price (the anchor), then justifying the value, beats
the reverse pattern of building up to the price.

**Status:** Acme renewal is the trial. SOW v2 leads with "+18%" on the
first page, with justification on page 2. Will compare against Beta-Corp
renewal in Q3 (where pricing came up at the end of a long value pitch).

### H3 (fresh): Part-time admin hire pays for itself within 8 weeks

Hypothesis: 5-10h/week of admin (invoicing, scheduling, light comms) at
$30-40/h frees 5-10h/week of consulting time at $200+/h. Should pay for
itself in under 8 weeks.

**Status:** untested. Decision doc in draft. Earliest start: June 1 if
greenlit by mid-May.

### H4 (active): Friday retros catch scope creep before it hardens

Hypothesis: weekly retros surface "I said yes to a small thing on
Tuesday that's now eating my Thursday" patterns early enough to push
back.

**Status:** holding. Two scope-creep instances caught at retro this
quarter; one rolled back, one converted to written scope expansion.

## Retired hypotheses

### H5 (retired 2026-04-13): Pursue all client types until Q2 economics are clearer

Hypothesis from Q1 launch: stay open to SaaS, fintech, e-commerce — find
the highest-leverage segment.

**Status retired:** Q1 data was decisive. Continuing the open mix would
be opportunity cost. Replaced by H1.

## Principles

- **Verify SLA in writing before starting any new deliverable.** Beta-Corp
  scope creep showed the cost.
- **Friday is sacred for retro and personal slot — no client EOW pushes
  scheduled Friday afternoon by my own calendar.**
- **Three weekly priorities cap.** Not 5, not 4. If a fourth thing
  appears, something gets renegotiated.
- **Decisions over $5K (revenue or expense) get a written decision doc
  before action.** Acme renewal is at this threshold.
